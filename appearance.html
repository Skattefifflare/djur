APPEARANCE
The white Bengal tiger is a rare color variant of the Bengal tiger. It has a white or pale cream coat with black, gray, or brown stripes. Its fur lacks the orange pigment due to a genetic mutation called leucism, distinct from albinism. White Bengal tigers typically have blue or green eyes and are often larger than their orange counterparts, with more pronounced musculature. Their striking appearance is a result of selective breeding in captivity.
